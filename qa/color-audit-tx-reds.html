#tx-enable-btn.tx-btn-armed recoloured: #f85149 → #803030

Terminal ANSI escapes don't render in this session's console, so the earlier attempt to show swatches came through as literal [48;2;…m text rather than colour. Below is the proper before/after render, plus the HSL numbers explaining why the old pair was genuinely hard to tell apart — it wasn't just a rendering problem — and why the new pair isn't.

Naming, resolved. web/js/main.js:57 itself calls #f85149 (armed) the "dark-red" and #ff3b30 (actively transmitting) the "bright-red" — so that pair is what "dark vs. bright red" refers to. A third, unrelated colour (rgb(180,40,40), the decode-row highlight) is separately called "dark red" in app.css:1016 — a coincidental reuse of the same words for a different element, addressed at the bottom.

Same hue, both near-maximum saturation. The only difference was 4 points of lightness — roughly the gap between "red" and "slightly lighter red." At button size, that gap was close to imperceptible. Not a display or terminal problem — the colours themselves were this close.

Roughly 3.7× the colour distance. Armed is now a muted, desaturated brick red; transmitting stays the original vivid red. Distinct in hue-neighbourhood feel (dull vs. vivid) as well as raw numbers, not just a lightness nudge.
